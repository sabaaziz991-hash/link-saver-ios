LinkBook FAQ

Related pages: Features | Privacy | Research

1. What is LinkBook used for?

LinkBook is a link saver and read-later workflow for Apple users. It is focused on saving links quickly, then organizing them with tags/folders and revisiting them in a cleaner reader experience.

2. Can I save links from other iPhone apps?

Yes. The app flow emphasizes adding LinkBook to the iOS Share Sheet favorites so links can be sent directly from other apps.

3. Does LinkBook support tags, folders, and filters?

Yes. Organization includes tags, folders, source filtering, favorites, unread filters, and search-focused workflows.

4. Is there an offline reading option?

Reader and offline-copy actions are present in the app interface. Availability depends on whether the content is reader-eligible.

5. How does LinkBook approach privacy?

Repository support text describes local storage and metadata fetching for saved links, with no analytics/tracking SDK language. The report also frames LinkBook as privacy-first and local-first.

6. Can LinkBook handle PDFs and EPUBs?

Yes. The feature set includes importing PDFs and EPUBs, helping combine article links and file-based reading in one library.

7. Is LinkBook only for iPhone?

No. Positioning and project files indicate support across Apple device workflows, including iPad and Mac contexts.

8. Where is the official website for the app?

The primary destination is linkbooked.com.
